# Industry Patterns

Per-industry guidance for the visual thesis, copy voice, CTAs, and the signature visual
section. Find the closest match to the user's business and use it as a **starting point,
not a script** — the thesis must still be specific to this brand. If the industry isn't
listed, interpolate from the two nearest and say so in `SITE_BRIEF.md`.

Every pattern below deliberately differs from the others in palette temperature, type
personality, motion character, and signature concept. If your draft site would look the
same under a different industry's header, the thesis failed — start over.

---

## Restaurant / fine dining
- **World:** warm and material — charcoal, ember, cream, smoke; or bright natural light
  for daytime concepts. Photography-forward; texture (linen, wood grain, char) over flat
  color.
- **Type:** expressive serif or high-contrast display; menus set with tabular care.
- **Copy voice:** sensory and specific ("12 hours over oak embers"), never "culinary
  excellence" clichés. Short lines, appetite first.
- **CTAs:** reserve a table (WhatsApp/booking link), view menu, private events.
- **Signature section ideas:** the tasting sequence as a scroll timeline; an ingredient
  map of sourcing; the fire/prep cycle; a dish reveal with slow crossfades.
- **Trust:** chef story, awards if real, press quotes (placeholder-marked).
- **Avoid:** stock food photos with mismatched lighting, delivery-app energy, dark+gold
  luxury clichés unless the concept truly is old-world luxury.

## Dental / medical clinic
- **World:** calm light mode — bone white, soft mineral tones, one reassuring accent
  (deep teal, muted sky). Air and order; generous whitespace reads as hygiene and calm.
- **Type:** humanist sans, gentle weights; large friendly numerals for stats/steps.
- **Copy voice:** plain-language reassurance; explain procedures without jargon; lead
  with how patients feel, not equipment specs.
- **CTAs:** book an appointment, WhatsApp the front desk, emergency line (distinct).
- **Signature section ideas:** the patient journey (arrive → assess → treat → smile) as
  a soft-motion storyline; an interactive smile/anatomy diagram (tasteful, schematic);
  before/after slider only with real, consented imagery — otherwise schematic.
- **Trust:** credentials, association memberships (only if provided), safety protocols,
  real reviews — all placeholder-marked otherwise. Never invent medical claims or
  guarantee outcomes.
- **Avoid:** dark cinematic drama, urgency tactics, gold accents, stocky smiling-people
  clichés, anything that reads as spa-luxury when the brief says clinical care.

## Law firm
- **World:** restrained authority — deep ink or slate on warm paper tones; one somber
  accent (oxblood, forest, navy). Light or dark both work if disciplined.
- **Type:** editorial serif for display (gravitas), crisp grotesque for body; hairline
  rules and numbered structure echo legal documents.
- **Copy voice:** precise, declarative, zero hype. Confidence through specificity of
  practice areas, not adjectives.
- **CTAs:** schedule a consultation, call directly; email prominent. WhatsApp only if
  the firm actually uses it.
- **Signature section ideas:** practice areas as an indexed document system; a case
  process timeline (intake → strategy → resolution); a typographic statement wall.
- **Trust:** bar admissions, years, notable matters — strictly provided-or-placeholder.
  Jurisdiction disclaimers matter; add a "not legal advice" footer line.
- **Avoid:** gavels/scales stock clichés, aggressive "we fight for you" tone unless
  requested, dark+gold rental-car luxury styling.

## SaaS / tech product
- **World:** product-first. Light editorial or deep-tech dark — pick from the product's
  own UI so screenshots sit naturally. One brand accent; no rainbow gradients.
- **Type:** contemporary grotesque or neo-grotesque; monospace details for technical
  credibility (metrics, code, API snippets).
- **Copy voice:** outcome-led and concrete ("close the books in 3 days, not 3 weeks");
  feature names in plain words. Kill buzzwords ("empower", "seamless", "supercharge").
- **CTAs:** start free / book a demo (pick ONE primary), docs or product tour secondary.
- **Signature section ideas:** the product itself — an annotated UI walkthrough that
  assembles on scroll; a live-feeling data/metric animation; an integration/system
  diagram drawing itself; a before/after workflow comparison.
- **Trust:** logos only if real; metrics placeholder-marked; security/compliance badges
  only if provided (SOC2 claims are legal claims).
- **Avoid:** three-generic-cards-with-icons, purple-blue gradient hero, fake dashboard
  screenshots pretending to be the product, cinematic car-ad drama.

## Architecture / interior design
- **World:** gallery restraint — near-white or warm plaster field, ink text, imagery
  does the talking. Large negative space; the grid itself is the aesthetic.
- **Type:** refined grotesque or quiet serif, small and disciplined; oversized only for
  project names. Captions and indices (01, 02) everywhere.
- **Copy voice:** spare and material ("light, concrete, patience"); let projects speak;
  first-person studio voice works.
- **CTAs:** start a project conversation, view portfolio; email over WhatsApp usually.
- **Signature section ideas:** a floor-plan that draws itself; section/elevation lines
  animating over a photo; a materials palette board; project scroll with plan-to-photo
  transitions.
- **Trust:** selected clients, publications, awards — provided-or-placeholder.
- **Avoid:** heavy motion (buildings don't bounce), dark luxury gradients, generic
  "we design dreams" copy, cluttered galleries.

## Fitness / wellness
- **World:** two honest directions — energetic (high-contrast, dark base, one electric
  accent, bold diagonal energy) or restorative (warm neutrals, sage/sand, soft light).
  Pick from the brand's actual promise; never mix both.
- **Type:** condensed impact type for energetic; airy humanist for restorative.
- **Copy voice:** second person, action verbs, honest about effort ("show up; we do the
  rest" beats "transform your life").
- **CTAs:** claim a trial class / book a session (WhatsApp fine), see schedule.
- **Signature section ideas:** a week-in-the-life schedule that animates; a training
  method broken into phases with a progress line; breath/pulse-rhythm motion for
  wellness; a results wall (placeholder-marked).
- **Trust:** coach credentials, member counts (placeholder-marked), class guarantees
  only if real.
- **Avoid:** shredded-torso stock clichés, before/after pressure tactics for wellness
  brands, luxury-car dark+gold styling.

## Semiconductor / electronics / deep tech
- **World:** precision-dark or clean-lab light — graphite, silicon blue-grey, one
  signal accent (copper, phosphor green). Schematic aesthetics: grids, traces, pins.
- **Type:** engineering grotesque + monospace for part numbers, specs, tolerances.
- **Copy voice:** spec-literate and sober; engineers smell marketing fluff instantly.
  Numbers with units beat adjectives.
- **CTAs:** request datasheet / talk to engineering / request samples. Email or form;
  WhatsApp rarely fits.
- **Signature section ideas:** a PCB/die trace that routes itself on scroll; an exploded
  package/stack diagram; a signal waveform animating through a pipeline; a fab process
  timeline (design → tapeout → validation).
- **Trust:** certifications and compliance only if provided; quality process; logistics
  reach. Placeholder-mark everything else.
- **Avoid:** consumer-lifestyle imagery, cinematic emotional copy, any invented spec or
  certification (these are legal claims), car-showroom styling.

## Local service (plumbing, electrical, cleaning, landscaping…)
- **World:** trustworthy daylight — clean light base, one strong dependable accent
  (safety orange, work-van blue). Real-work texture beats gloss; premium here means
  "organized and reliable", not "luxurious".
- **Type:** sturdy grotesque, big readable sizes; phone numbers set large.
- **Copy voice:** direct neighbor-to-neighbor ("we show up when we say"); service areas
  and response times explicit.
- **CTAs:** call now + WhatsApp (both prominent, sticky on mobile), request a quote.
- **Signature section ideas:** service-area map (only if geography genuinely matters —
  not as a default); a "what happens when you call" step strip; an honest pricing
  structure panel; a day-of-service timeline.
- **Trust:** licensed/insured (placeholder-marked unless provided), guarantees, review
  snippets (placeholder-marked).
- **Avoid:** cinematic drama that undermines approachability, dark mode, luxury
  styling that makes prices look scary, fake "24/7" claims.

## Luxury automotive (rental, detailing, dealership)
- **World:** this is the ONE place dark cinematic + warm metal accent genuinely fits —
  and even here, differentiate: champagne vs. carbon vs. porcelain showroom light.
  Machine textures: paint depth, brushed metal, asphalt at dusk.
- **Type:** wide editorial display with tight tracking; tabular numerals for specs.
- **Copy voice:** restrained desire; specifics of the machines and the service ritual,
  not "experience luxury" filler.
- **CTAs:** WhatsApp concierge/booking, browse fleet/inventory.
- **Signature section ideas:** fleet rail with layered depth; a delivery-zone map (fits
  here); a spec sheet that assembles; paint-surface macro with light sweep.
- **Trust:** insurance/licensing placeholder-marked; delivery logistics; concierge SLA.
- **Avoid:** copying any reference site's structure verbatim; visible third-party
  brand logos in generated imagery; fake plates.

## Real estate (brokerage, development, property management)
- **World:** light and aspirational for residential (warm white, honey wood, sky);
  darker composed tone acceptable for high-end development brands. Architectural
  photography leads.
- **Type:** elegant serif or quiet grotesque; prices and areas in tabular numerals.
- **Copy voice:** neighborhood-literate and concrete (light, layout, location); selling
  a life pattern, not "dream homes".
- **CTAs:** schedule a viewing (WhatsApp fine), browse listings, valuation request.
- **Signature section ideas:** a neighborhood/portfolio map (fits when location is the
  product); a floor-plan-to-photo reveal; a buying-process timeline; a day-in-the-light
  sequence of one property.
- **Trust:** portfolio numbers, years, certifications — placeholder-marked; legal/broker
  ID lines where jurisdictions require them.
- **Avoid:** inventing listings that look real (mark fictional properties clearly),
  golden-hour clichés on every image, luxury-car styling for family brokerages.

---

## Using a pattern

1. Read the matching pattern before writing the visual thesis.
2. Take the **direction**, then make it this brand's own: the palette values, type
   pairing, signature concept, and copy must come from the brief, not from this file.
3. Record in `SITE_BRIEF.md` which pattern you started from and what you changed.
